## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 "Confidential Information"

All non-public information disclosed by a party, whether oral, written,
electronic, visual, or in any other form, including but not limited to:
source code, technical documentation, inventions, prototypes, customer
lists, pricing, financial data, due diligence materials, architectural
diagrams, security policies, and any information reasonably understood
to be confidential.

### 1.2 "Deliverables"

All reports, software, documentation, analyses, or other materials
provided by Vendor to Client under a Statement of Work ("SOW").

### 1.3 "Services"

The professional, technical, or advisory services that Vendor provides
under this Agreement or any SOW.

### 1.4 "Background IP"

Intellectual property owned or controlled by a party prior to the
Effective Date or developed independently outside the scope of this
Agreement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Scope of Services

Vendor shall provide Services and Deliverables as described in one or
more mutually agreed-upon SOWs executed under this Agreement. Each SOW
will specify the applicable fees, milestones, performance requirements,
acceptance criteria, and timelines.

Vendor shall perform the Services: - in a professional and workmanlike
manner; - using qualified personnel; and - in accordance with industry
best practices and Client's written policies, including information
security standards referenced in **Exhibit C**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Fees and Payment

### 3.1 Fees

Client shall pay Vendor the fees specified in each SOW.

### 3.2 Invoices

Vendor shall issue invoices no more frequently than monthly and must
reference the applicable SOW number, period of performance, and itemized
charges.

### 3.3 Payment Terms

Payments are due within **net 30 days** of Client's receipt of an
undisputed invoice.

### 3.4 Expenses

Pre-approved, reasonable travel and incidental expenses will be
reimbursed in accordance with Client's travel policy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Ownership

-   **Client Materials:** All rights in Client's Background IP remain
    solely with Client.\
-   **Vendor Materials:** Vendor retains all rights in its Background
    IP.\
-   **Deliverables:** Upon full payment, Vendor assigns to Client all
    rights in the Deliverables, excluding Vendor Background IP embedded
    therein.

### 4.2 License to Vendor Background IP

Vendor grants Client a **non-exclusive, non-transferable, limited
license** to use Vendor Background IP solely as incorporated into the
Deliverables, and solely for Client's internal business purposes.

### 4.3 Sublicensing

Client may not sublicense Vendor Background IP to any third party
without Vendor's **prior written consent**, except to Client's
affiliates.

### 4.4 Restrictions

Client shall not: - reverse engineer Vendor Background IP (except as
permitted by law); - remove proprietary notices; or - use Vendor
Background IP for benchmarking.

### 4.5 Independent Development

Nothing restricts either party from independently developing similar
products or services, provided no Confidential Information is used.

### 4.6 Residuals

Subject to **Section 7**, each party may use residual know‑how retained
in unaided memory of personnel.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Acceptance of Deliverables

Deliverables are deemed accepted upon the earliest of: 1. Client's
written acceptance;\
2. Client's use in production;\
3. Ten (10) business days after delivery without written rejection.

Vendor will correct any non‑conformities at no additional cost.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Representations and Warranties

Vendor represents that: - It has authority to enter the Agreement. -
Services will conform to SOW requirements. - Deliverables will not
knowingly infringe IP rights. - It will comply with all laws.

All other warranties are disclaimed.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Confidentiality

### 7.1 Obligations

Each party shall: - protect the other's Confidential Information; - use
it only for purposes of this Agreement; - disclose it only to personnel
with a need to know.

### 7.2 Security Standards

Vendor must comply with **Exhibit C -- Information Security
Requirements**, as updated.

### 7.3 Survival

Confidentiality obligations survive **five (5) years**; trade secrets
survive indefinitely.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Term and Termination

-   Agreement lasts **three (3) years** unless earlier terminated.\
-   Either party may terminate for convenience with 30 days' notice.\
-   Immediate termination allowed for material breach not cured in 20
    days.\
-   Client pays for Services through termination date.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Indemnification

Vendor indemnifies Client for third‑party claims involving: - IP
infringement; - Vendor negligence or misconduct; - Breach of
confidentiality.

Client indemnifies Vendor for misuse of Deliverables.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. Limitation of Liability

No party is liable for indirect or consequential damages.\
Total liability capped at fees paid in the prior 12 months.

## Exhibit C -- Information Security Requirements (Summary)

Vendor must: 1. Maintain SOC 2 Type II controls or equivalent.\
2. Encrypt data in transit and at rest.\
3. Enforce MFA for admin access.\
4. Notify Client of incidents within 24 hours.\
5. Participate in annual security assessments.
