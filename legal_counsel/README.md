# Legal Counsel Demo Dataset

## About This Dataset

This directory contains **synthetic legal documents** created for demonstration purposes. All company names, parties, terms, and agreements are entirely fictional. These documents are designed to resemble authentic legal materials while containing no actual confidential or proprietary information.

## Purpose

These materials are crafted to:
- Be immediately recognizable and relatable to legal professionals
- Demonstrate Semiont's capabilities for legal document analysis and annotation
- Show realistic legal review workflows and correspondence
- Illustrate how legal concepts, obligations, and relationships can be systematically annotated and extracted

## Contents

The synthetic documents include:
- **Master Services Agreement (MSA)** - A draft contract between Acme Corporation and a vendor with redline revisions
- **Legal Email** - Attorney correspondence discussing contract revisions, IP licensing, and confidentiality provisions
- **Internal Letter** - Legal counsel communication about contract review deadlines and due diligence

## Legal Realism

While entirely fictional, these documents incorporate:
- Standard legal language and formatting conventions
- Common contractual clauses and provisions (confidentiality, IP licensing, indemnification)
- Realistic business scenarios and review workflows
- Typical attorney-client correspondence patterns
- References to property verification and due diligence processes

## Usage

These materials are ideal for:
- Testing extraction of legal entities (parties, attorneys, addresses, dates)
- Identifying contractual terms and obligations
- Tracking document revision history and redline changes
- Extracting key deadlines and action items
- Mapping relationships between related legal documents
- Demonstrating real-world legal review workflows

## Important Disclaimer

**These documents are synthetic training materials only.** They:
- Should NOT be used as legal templates or forms
- Do NOT constitute legal advice
- Have NOT been reviewed by legal professionals for accuracy
- Are NOT suitable for any actual legal purpose

They are purely educational tools designed to demonstrate natural language processing and information extraction techniques on legal-domain content.

## Target Audience

These materials are designed to be familiar to:
- Corporate legal departments
- Law firm attorneys
- Contract managers and analysts
- Legal operations professionals
- Compliance officers
- Anyone involved in contract negotiation and review

The language, structure, and scenarios reflect typical corporate legal practice to ensure the demonstrations are relevant and meaningful to legal professionals.
